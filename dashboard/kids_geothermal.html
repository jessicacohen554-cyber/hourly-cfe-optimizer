How Geothermal Energy Works

The Earth is hot inside! Let’s use that heat for clean power.

Ages 5 – 7

Heat From Deep Underground!

Did you know the Earth is super hot on the inside? Deep below your feet, it’s so hot that rocks actually melt! We can use that heat to make electricity.

The Earth Is Like a Giant Oven!

It’s hot down there!

The Earth is made of layers — like an onion! On the outside it’s cool (that’s where we live). But deep down, it gets hotter and hotter.

At the very center, the Earth’s core is as hot as the surface of the sun — about 10,000 degrees! That heat is called geothermal energy (“geo” means earth, “thermal” means heat).

🌋 Fun fact: If you could dig a deep enough hole, the ground gets hotter by about 25°C for every kilometer you go down!

Geysers: Earth’s Hot Fountains!

Sometimes the Earth’s heat makes water underground get really, really hot. When it boils, it shoots up through cracks in the ground. That’s a geyser!

You might have heard of Old Faithful in Yellowstone Park — it shoots hot water 130 feet into the air! Hot springs and geysers are nature’s way of showing us there’s energy underground.

♨ Fun fact: In Iceland, people heat their homes with hot water from underground. Some pools are naturally warm enough to swim in — even in winter!

Turning Heat Into Electricity!

Cold water goes down
Hot water comes up!

To make electricity, we drill deep holes into the ground and pump cold water down. The hot rocks heat the water up. Then the hot water (or steam) comes back up!

That steam spins a turbine — just like in a nuclear or coal plant — and the turbine makes electricity. But there’s no burning, no smoke, and no CO₂!

Best of all, geothermal works all day, all night, rain or shine — just like nuclear. The Earth’s heat never stops!

🔥 Key idea: Geothermal energy is always on, always clean, and will last for billions of years. The Earth is like a giant rechargeable battery!
