Where Does Our Power Come From?

Every time you turn on a light, charge a tablet, or open the fridge, you’re using electricity. But where does it come from? Let’s find out!

The Sun: Our Biggest Power Source

Solar panels catch sunlight and turn it into electricity — no smoke, no noise, no pollution. They work best on sunny days.

One hour of sunshine hitting the Earth has enough energy to power the whole world for a year! We just need to catch it.

☀️ Fun fact: The sun is so far away that its light takes 8 minutes to reach us — but it still powers millions of homes!

Wind: Invisible Energy

Wind turbines have giant blades that spin when the wind blows. That spinning motion makes electricity — like a bicycle dynamo, but much bigger!

Some wind turbines are as tall as the Statue of Liberty. Their blades can be longer than a Boeing 747 wing.

🌀 Fun fact: One big wind turbine can make enough electricity to power about 1,500 homes!

Water: The Original Clean Power

Hydropower uses flowing water to spin turbines. Dams hold back rivers and release water through generators to make electricity.

People have used moving water for power for thousands of years — first to grind grain, now to light up cities.

💧 Fun fact: The Hoover Dam makes enough electricity to serve over a million people!

Batteries: Saving Energy for Later

⚡
Charge & release!

The sun doesn’t always shine and the wind doesn’t always blow. That’s why we need batteries — giant ones! — to store energy and use it later.

Think of it like a water bottle: you fill it up when there’s water, and drink from it when you’re thirsty.

🔋 Fun fact: Some grid batteries are as big as a football field and can power thousands of homes for hours!

The Big Challenge: What Happens at Night?

When the sun goes down, solar panels stop working.
If the wind isn’t blowing either, we need another plan!

This is called the “8,760 Problem” — there are 8,760 hours in a year, and we need clean energy for every single one.

Some hours are easy (sunny, windy afternoons). Some are hard (still, cloudy winter nights). Solving the hard hours is the big puzzle!

Teamwork Makes Clean Energy Work!

No single energy source can do it alone. We need solar + wind + water + batteries working together like a team.

Solar covers the daytime. Wind often blows at night. Water can ramp up quickly. Batteries fill in the gaps. Together, they can power everything — cleanly!

🌎 Key idea: Clean energy is a team sport. The more sources we combine, the closer we get to powering the world without pollution.
