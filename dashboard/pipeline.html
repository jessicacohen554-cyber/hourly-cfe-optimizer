About the Model

The 8,760 Problem Optimizer

A procurement-focused hourly CFE optimizer that co-optimizes resource mix and cost across 9 sensitivity dimensions. Full 8,760-hour resolution, 5 U.S. ISOs, >5,000 cost scenarios per region. The model takes the grid as-given and answers: what portfolio of clean energy should a buyer procure to meet hourly matching targets at minimum cost? Not a system planning model — a procurement tool built on observed EIA data anchored to a 2025 snapshot.

Core Pipeline

Steps 1–4 run sequentially. Step 1 is compute-intensive (hours); Steps 2–4 are cheap (seconds to minutes). Step 5 post-processors run in parallel. Step 6 exports all data for the dashboard.

1

PFS Generator

~4 hrs

step1_pfs_generator.py — Generates the Physics Feasible Space (PFS). 4D adaptive grid search over clean_firm, solar, wind, and hydro resource fractions with procurement sweep. Battery daily-cycle dispatch (4hr, 85% RTE) and LDES multi-day dispatch (100hr, 50% RTE). Produces 21.4M physics-validated resource mixes across 13 thresholds and 5 ISOs.

Only re-run if dispatch logic, generation curves, or demand curves change.

IN data/eia_generation_profiles.json, data/eia_demand_profiles.json
OUT data/physics_cache_v4.parquet
OUT data/checkpoints_v4/ (incremental checkpoints)
1.5

Storage Re-Sweep

~10 min

postprocess_storage_resweep.py — Re-dispatches battery/LDES for mixes near EF boundary with finer procurement grid. Appends improved solutions to PFS cache.

IN data/physics_cache_v4.parquet
OUT data/physics_cache_v4.parquet (appended rows)
2

Efficient Frontier

~30 sec

step2_efficient_frontier.py — Extracts the Efficient Frontier (EF) from the PFS. Filters by existing generation utilization, procurement minimization, and strict dominance removal. Reduces 21.4M solutions to ~1.8M Pareto-optimal mixes.

IN data/physics_cache_v4.parquet
OUT data/pfs_post_ef.parquet
3

Cost Optimization

~20 min

step3_cost_optimization.py — Vectorized cross-evaluation of EF mixes under 5,832 cost sensitivity combos per region (17,496 for CAISO). Merit-order tranche pricing for clean firm: uprate → geothermal → cheapest of nuclear/CCS. Demand growth sweep across 25 years × 3 growth rates.

IN data/pfs_post_ef.parquet
OUT dashboard/overprocure_results.json
OUT dashboard/overprocure_scenarios.parquet
OUT data/optimizer_cache.json
4

Post-Processing

~60 sec

step4_postprocess.py — Applies NEISO winter gas pipeline constraint (+$13.13/MWh CCS adder), 45Q tax credit correction ($27.5/MWh), without-45Q overlay, gas capacity backup pricing, and resource adequacy margin (15% RA). Also runs CCS vs LDES crossover analysis.

IN dashboard/overprocure_results.json
OUT dashboard/overprocure_results.json (corrected)
5

Post-Processors

Run independently in parallel after Step 4. Each reads from Step 3/4 outputs and produces specialized analysis data.

CO₂ Recompute

~15 sec

recompute_co2.py — Dispatch-stack emission model. Merit-order retirement: coal first, then oil, then gas. Fast path uses pre-computed hourly_match_score (~1000x speedup). Numba-accelerated fallback for full hourly dispatch.

IN dashboard/overprocure_results.json
IN data/egrid_emission_rates.json, data/eia_fossil_mix.json
OUT dashboard/overprocure_results.json (CO₂ fields added)

MAC Statistics

~30 sec

compute_mac_stats.py — Computes 6 MAC metrics: average fan (P10/P50/P90), stepwise marginal, monotonic envelope, path-constrained. ANOVA sensitivity decomposition across 5 toggle groups. Crossover analysis vs DAC/SCC/ETS.

IN dashboard/overprocure_results.json
OUT dashboard/js/mac-stats-data.js

LMP Pricing

~10 sec

compute_lmp_prices.py — Wholesale energy market simulation. Dispatch-stack LMP model with merit-order fuel retirement. Computes price distributions, nuclear breakeven analysis, and revenue adequacy metrics.

IN dashboard/overprocure_results.json
IN data/eia_generation_profiles.json, data/eia_demand_profiles.json
OUT dashboard/lmp_analysis_results.json

Track Optimization

~5 min

temp_track.py — Runs cost optimization for NB (new-build) and CTR (cost-to-replace) analysis tracks. Same EF mixes, different cost assumptions and resource constraints.

Compressed Day Profiles

~10 sec

step5_compressed_day.py — Generates 24-hour representative day profiles from the full 8,760-hour dispatch data. Compact supply/demand curves for dashboard visualization of daily dispatch patterns.

Shared Data Export

generate_shared_data.py — Final pipeline step. Extracts all results into a single JavaScript module for the interactive dashboard. SBTi milestone mapping, DAC trajectory projections, LCOE/transmission tables for client-side repricing. All dashboard pages read from this file.

Analysis Tracks

Three analysis tracks share the same Physics Feasible Space but apply different resource constraints and cost assumptions. All tracks read from data/pfs_post_ef.parquet.

Baseline

Standard Optimization

Full co-optimization with all resource types available. The primary analysis track used across the dashboard.

  • All resources available: solar, wind, nuclear, CCS, hydro, battery, LDES
  • Hydro capped at existing regional capacity
  • Nuclear uprates included at reduced cost
  • 5,832 cost scenarios per ISO/threshold
  • Script: step3_cost_optimization.py
New-Build (NB)

No Existing Resources

What would a greenfield buyer build from scratch? Removes hydro (existing-only) and nuclear uprates.

  • Hydro forced to 0% (not available for new-build)
  • Nuclear uprates OFF — only new-build nuclear at full LCOE
  • All other resources at new-build pricing
  • Shows the “pure market” optimum
  • Script: temp_track.py (NB mode)
Cost to Replace (CTR)

Existing Fleet Replacement

What does it cost to replace existing nuclear with alternatives? All resources at existing fleet prices, no uprates.

  • All resources modeled as existing fleet (wholesale pricing)
  • Nuclear uprates OFF — existing capacity only
  • Hydro at existing regional capacity
  • Answers “what’s the cost if we lose this plant?”
  • Script: temp_track.py (CTR mode)

Shared dispatch engine used by recompute_co2.py, compute_lmp_prices.py, and step5_compressed_day.py. Contains Numba-compiled battery and LDES dispatch loops, supply matrix construction, dispatch cache system, and merit-order fossil retirement model.

  • ■ build_supply_matrix() — Pre-built (5, 8760) numpy array per ISO
  • ■ compute_fossil_retirement() — Merit-order emission rate at threshold

How It Compares

The optimizer sits in a distinct niche — more granular than system planning models, more rigorous than LCOE screens.

vs. Capacity Expansion Models

GenX, ReEDS, SWITCH, PLEXOS

Capacity expansion models optimize the entire power system — generation, transmission, retirements, and entry — over multi-decade horizons. They answer “what should the grid build?” We answer “what should a buyer procure today?” Our model takes the existing grid as fixed and optimizes procurement portfolios within it. We share the same hourly resolution as GenX (8,760 hours) but focus on cost sensitivity and portfolio co-optimization rather than system planning.

vs. Economy-Wide / IAMs

GCAM, TIMES, REMIND, E3ME

Integrated assessment models simulate entire economies — energy, land use, industry, transport — over century timescales. They trade temporal resolution for scope: annual or 5-year timesteps vs. our hourly granularity. We don’t model cross-sector feedbacks, GDP impacts, or macro-economic equilibria. We go deep on one question — hourly clean energy procurement — with a level of temporal and cost-sensitivity detail that economy-wide models can’t support.

vs. LCOE / Screening Curves

Lazard, NREL ATB, EIA AEO levelized costs

LCOE analysis compares technologies on a $/MWh basis but ignores when power is generated. Solar at $30/MWh is meaningless if you need power at midnight. We enforce hourly matching — every MWh of demand must be met in the hour it occurs. This is why our optimal mixes include storage and firm generation that LCOE screening would discard as “too expensive.”

vs. Dispatch / Production Cost Models

PLEXOS (dispatch), GE MAPS, PROMOD, Aurora

Production cost models simulate detailed unit commitment — minimum generation constraints, ramp rates, start-up costs, ancillary services. We use simplified dispatch (capacity-constrained, no unit commitment) because our objective is portfolio cost optimization across thousands of sensitivity scenarios, not operational simulation of a single grid state. We trade dispatch fidelity for breadth of cost exploration.

What It Can & Can’t Do

Strengths

  • Full 8,760-hour matching — not reduced time-slices or representative weeks. Captures seasonal nuclear derates, summer solar peaks, winter wind patterns
  • Cost + mix co-optimization — re-optimizes the resource portfolio for every cost scenario. Reveals when cost assumptions change the optimal strategy, not just the price
  • 9-dimensional parametric sensitivity with >5,000 scenarios per region. Shows the full uncertainty landscape, not a single “base case”
  • Merit-order clean firm tranche pricing — uprates fill first (cheapest), then geothermal (CAISO), then new nuclear or CCS (whichever cheaper). Reflects real supply curves
  • Two-tier pricing — existing capacity at regional wholesale, new-build at LCOE + transmission. Captures the procurement reality that existing is cheap and new is expensive
  • Storage dispatch with physical constraints — battery (4hr daily cycle, 85% RTE) and LDES (100hr multi-day, 50% RTE) both capacity-limited. No unrealistic perfect-foresight dispatch
  • CO₂ dispatch attribution with charge netting — tracks exact dispatch hours and nets charge-side emissions. GHG Protocol Scope 2 compliant
  • Policy analysis — 45Q tax credit toggle, demand growth sweep, NB/CTR track comparison. Answers “what happens if this policy changes?”
  • Regional specificity — 5 ISOs with distinct generation profiles, demand shapes, wholesale prices, and fossil fuel mixes. Not a national average

Limitations

  • No transmission expansion or congestion — uses existing interconnection costs as static adders. Doesn’t model whether transmission can physically deliver the resources
  • No inter-regional trade — each ISO is self-contained. Understates feasibility for import-dependent regions (e.g., NEISO could import Canadian hydro)
  • No unit commitment — simplified dispatch without minimum generation, ramp rates, or start-up costs. May overstate flexibility of firm resources
  • No reliability / ELCC constraints — mixes meet hourly energy matching but don’t enforce peak capacity reserve margins. High-solar mixes may technically match hours but lack capacity adequacy
  • Static 2025 snapshot — no multi-year buildout trajectory, learning curves, or retirement schedules. Can’t answer “when should I build?”
  • Fixed demand profiles — no demand response, load flexibility, or electrification shifts. Demand shapes are immutable inputs
  • No hydrogen storage — excluded as commercially immature. May understate long-duration options for extreme scenarios
  • Static LDES cost — LCOS is fixed regardless of utilization. Low-utilization scenarios may understate true cost; high-utilization may overstate it
  • CCS modeled as baseload under 45Q — assumes 100% capacity factor when 45Q is active (economic incentive to maximize capture). May overstate CCS output in shoulder hours
