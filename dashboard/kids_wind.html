How Wind Energy Works

Whoooosh! Let’s learn about the power of wind!

Ages 5 – 7

The Wind Is Full of Energy!

You can’t see the wind, but you can feel it! It blows your hair, flies kites, and pushes sailboats. But did you know it can also make electricity?

Feel the Breeze!

So windy!

Wind is air that moves. When the sun heats up the ground, warm air rises and cool air rushes in to take its place. That moving air is wind!

People have used wind for thousands of years — to sail ships and grind wheat. Now we use it to make electricity!

🌀 Fun fact: Wind can travel over 200 miles per hour in a tornado! But wind turbines work best with steady breezes of 15–25 mph.

How Does a Wind Turbine Work?

Blades catch the wind
Hub spins a generator
Tower holds it up high

A wind turbine has three big blades on top of a tall tower. When wind pushes the blades, they spin.

Inside the top part is a generator — a machine that turns spinning motion into electricity. It’s like a bicycle dynamo, but way bigger!

The electricity travels down the tower through wires and goes to your house.

💪 Fun fact: The blades of a big wind turbine are longer than a Boeing 747 wing — each one can be over 200 feet long!

Wind Farms: Teamwork!

One turbine is great, but lots of turbines together make a wind farm! Some wind farms have hundreds of turbines.

Wind farms can be on land (on hills and flat plains) or out at sea (called offshore wind farms). Offshore ones catch even stronger, steadier winds!

One big wind farm can make enough electricity for a whole city!

🌊 Fun fact: The biggest offshore wind farm in the world has over 150 turbines and powers more than a million homes!

What If the Wind Stops?

Zzz
No wind? No electricity!

Wind doesn’t blow all the time. On calm, still days, the turbine blades just sit there. No spinning = no electricity.

That’s why wind energy needs friends! When the wind stops, we can use solar panels, batteries, or other clean energy sources to keep the lights on.

Teamwork makes clean energy work!

🤔 Key idea: Wind is free and clean — it never runs out! But we need to combine it with other energy sources for the hours when it’s calm.
