How Nuclear Energy Works

Tiny atoms, HUGE power! Let’s explore the energy inside everything.

Ages 5 – 7

The Superpower Inside Atoms!

Everything around you — your toys, the air, even YOU — is made of teeny-tiny things called atoms. And atoms have a secret superpower: they hold a LOT of energy!

What Is an Atom?

An atom — everything is made of these!
So tiny!

An atom is the smallest piece of anything. They’re so small that millions of them could fit on the tip of your pencil!

In the middle of every atom is a tiny center called the nucleus. It’s packed with energy. When we split a nucleus apart, it releases a HUGE amount of heat!

That’s called nuclear energy — energy from splitting atoms.

🧬 Fun fact: One tiny piece of uranium (a special rock) the size of a marble has as much energy as a whole train car full of coal!

Inside a Nuclear Power Plant

A nuclear power plant splits atoms to make heat. That heat boils water into steam. The steam spins a big wheel called a turbine, and the turbine makes electricity!

The big towers you see are cooling towers. They cool down the steam so it turns back into water. The white puffs coming out are just steam — not smoke!

Nuclear plants don’t burn anything, so they make no CO₂ while running. That means clean air!

🏣 Fun fact: Those big cooling towers breathe out steam — just like your breath on a cold day! It’s water vapor, not pollution.

Day AND Night Power!

Nighttime? ON!

Here’s what makes nuclear energy really special: it works all the time! Day or night, rain or shine, windy or calm.

Solar panels need the sun. Wind turbines need the wind. But nuclear plants just keep going and going, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

That makes nuclear a great teammate for solar and wind — it fills in when they can’t!

💫 Key idea: Nuclear energy is one of the most powerful clean energy sources. A single plant can power a million homes — day and night, rain or shine!
