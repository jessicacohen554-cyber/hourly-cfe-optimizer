How Energy Storage Works

Saving sunshine for a rainy day! Learn how we store energy.

Ages 5 – 7

Saving Energy for Later!

What happens when the sun goes down or the wind stops? We need a way to save energy and use it later. That’s what batteries and storage do!

Why Do We Need to Store Energy?

⚡
Daytime: lots of power!
✗
Nighttime: no solar!
Save it!

During the day, solar panels can make more electricity than people need. But at night, they make none.

It’s like having lots of lemonade in the morning but nothing to drink at bedtime. If only we could save some for later…

That’s exactly what energy storage does! It saves extra energy when we have too much and gives it back when we need it.

💦 Fun fact: Think of storage like a water bottle — you fill it up at the fountain and drink it later when you’re thirsty!

How Do Batteries Work?

1
Sun shines, panels make electricity
2
Extra electricity charges the battery
3
At night, battery gives power back!

Batteries store electricity using special chemicals inside. When electricity flows in, the chemicals change one way. When you need power, they change back and push electricity out!

You already use batteries every day — in your tablet, your toys, and your parents’ phones. Grid batteries are the same idea, just MUCH bigger!

Some grid batteries are as big as a football field and can power thousands of homes for hours.

🔋 Fun fact: The biggest battery in the world is in Australia. It can store enough electricity to power 57,000 homes for an hour!

Other Ways to Store Energy!

Water pumped UP the hill
Water flows DOWN to make power
⚡
Grid battery

Batteries aren’t the only way to store energy! Here are some cool ideas:

Pumped hydro: When we have extra electricity, we pump water UP a hill into a lake. When we need power, we let the water flow DOWN through turbines. It’s like a giant water battery!

Iron-air batteries: These special batteries use iron (the stuff in nails!) and air to store energy for DAYS, not just hours. They’re great for cloudy weeks!

⛰ Fun fact: Pumped hydro storage has been around since the 1920s! It’s the oldest and most common way to store big amounts of energy.

Storage is the missing puzzle piece that makes clean energy work all the time!

Solar + wind + batteries = electricity for every single hour of the year — all 8,760 of them!

When we store energy, we don’t need to burn fossil fuels at night. The more storage we build, the cleaner our energy gets. And that means a happier, healthier planet!

🌎 Key idea: Energy storage is like having a piggy bank for electricity. Save it when you have extra, spend it when you need it!
